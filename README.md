# Web Techniques Project Documentation: BookVerse

## 1. Included Information
The proposed website, **BookVerse**, is a dedicated web platform for an online library. It allows users to browse, search, and purchase a wide variety of publications, ranging from scientific books and textbooks to magazines and promotional bundles. The goal is to provide a simple, well-organized, and intuitive experience for book lovers.

### User Experience:
* **Navigation:** Users can explore products by categories: Books (Scientific, Textbooks, Fiction) and Magazines (Technology, Culture, Lifestyle, Business).
* **Dynamic Discounts:** A system that calculates prices based on a "Trade-in" model for second-hand books.
* **Interactivity:** Users can filter products, manage their stock, and leave structured reviews.
* **Personalization:** A system for creating personalized publication bundles and receiving suggestions based on purchase history.
* **Trade-in System:** Users can trade in their old books for vouchers using the formula:
    `Voucher = (Base Price / 2) - Wear + Bonus`

## 2. Site Structure
The site is divided into main sections, accessible from a persistent header:
* **Home** – General presentation of the library, featured collections, and offers.
* **Shop/Catalog** – The core of the site where books and magazines are listed.
* **Trade-in (Voucher)** – A dedicated page where users can calculate the value of their used books.
* **About Us** – The mission of BookVerse and its story.
* **Contact** – Contact methods and store locations.
* **Cart** – Real-time management of selected items.

### Categories:
* **Publications:** Textbooks, Scientific, Fiction.
* **Magazines:** Tech, Medical, Economy, Arts, Fashion, Kids.
* **Subscriptions:** Specialized plans for recurring magazine deliveries.
